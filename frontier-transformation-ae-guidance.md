# Frontier Transformation Guidance for Account Executives (AEs)

> **Purpose**  
This document provides structured, role-specific guidance for **Account Executives (AEs)** to lead and drive the *Frontier Transformation* conversation with customers. It translates the ATU Fireside Chat talk track into an actionable playbook that AEs can use directly, or feed into **Copilot Cowork**, **GitHub Copilot**, or other agentic tools when:
- Preparing customer engagements
- Designing an interactive Frontier vision workshop
- Building customer-specific artifacts (briefings, agendas, roadmaps)

This guidance is designed to be **machine-readable, instruction-oriented, and reusable** as a system prompt or knowledge file for Copilot-based agents.

---

## 1. The AE’s Role in the Frontier Transformation Motion

### Core AE Accountability
The AE is the **orchestrator of the Frontier Transformation**. Their job is not to sell products first, but to:
- Open an **executive-level business conversation**
- Earn relevance as a **trusted advisor across the business** (not just IT)
- Align the full account team (ATU, CSA/STU, partners)
- Progress the customer from **outcomes → use cases → platform**

Frontier Transformation is not a pitch—it is a *directional business dialogue* that the AE must intentionally lead.

---

## 2. AE Preparation: Before the Customer Meeting

### 2.1 What “Good Preparation” Means for an AE

Preparation starts *before* any meeting is scheduled. The AE ensures they show up with **insight, not opinion**.

**AE preparation checklist:**
- Customer industry context and competitive pressures
- Market, regulatory, and macro trends affecting the customer
- Recent financial, strategic, or organizational signals
- Known business priorities and executive agenda items

AEs should actively leverage Copilot and agents (e.g., Researcher, Prompt Agent, Sales Agent) to build this view quickly and consistently.

### 2.2 Cross-CSA (STU) Actions – Pre-Meeting

The AE initiates and owns orchestration before the meeting:
- Engage **CSA/STU** early to validate:
  - Data, security, and AI maturity signals
  - Existing or planned AI / agentic initiatives (including BYOAI)
- Identify potential **foundation risks** (governance, security, silos)
- Align on which Frontier pillars are most relevant

The goal is *not* to design a solution yet—but to avoid walking in blind.

### 2.3 ATS (Partner) Actions – Pre-Meeting

The AE determines whether partners are needed *for credibility or context* (not execution yet):
- Industry-specific perspective
- Transformation or change management experience
- Platform-scale delivery credibility

Partners should be aligned on **outcomes and story**, not products.

---

## 3. Leading the Frontier Conversation: In the Meeting

### 3.1 How the AE Opens the Conversation

The AE opens **wide**, anchored in the CEO agenda:
- Industry forces and recognized tensions
- What “Frontier companies” look like in that industry
- Typical business outcomes they drive:
  - Growth
  - Customer experience
  - Cost efficiency

**Key discipline:**  
Do *not* open with Copilot, agents, or workloads. Frontier is the *frame*, not the solution.

---

### 3.2 The AE’s Listening Role

After framing, the AE must **create space to listen**:
- Encourage customer perspective
- Validate or refine assumed priorities
- Surface tension between current state and desired outcomes

The objective is shared understanding—*not agreement on products*.

---

## 4. From Outcomes to Use Cases (Without Losing Relevance)

### 4.1 Structuring What You Heard

The AE helps organize the discussion through:
- **Outcomes** (what success looks like)
- **Use cases** (where outcomes become tangible)
- **Frontier pillars**, viewed through:
  - Intelligence
  - Trust

This creates a **structured strategy**, not a loose conversation.

---

### 4.2 When the Platform Enters the Story

Platform is introduced only after outcomes and use cases are clear.

**AE framing:**
> “If these outcomes matter, we need to ensure the right foundation—so the first use case isn’t the last one that works.”

Platform is positioned as the **‘how’**, including:
- Data and knowledge foundations
- Security and governance
- Observability
- Agents and extensibility

This is the deliberate bridge from *Frontier vision* to *agentic platform*.

---

## 5. Handling Hard Scenarios

### 5.1 Customers Not Ready for ‘Big’ Transformation

Most customers already have:
- Fragmented AI or agent efforts
- Point solutions with limited scalability
- Security or governance gaps

**AE responsibility:**  
Expand the conversation beyond a single workload—otherwise the customer will transform one tool at a time.

---

### 5.2 Competitive Point Solutions

When competitors position something as faster or cheaper:

**What the AE should do:**
- Acknowledge the appeal (do not argue features)
- Reframe the tradeoff explicitly:
  - Speed vs. sustainability
  - Simplicity vs. future risk

**Suggested AE language:**
> “We can move fast on the first use case—and design the foundation so you’re ready for the next ones without creating silos or risk.”

---

## 6. Post-Conversation: What the AE Drives Next

### 6.1 Agreed Scenario and Stakeholders

The AE ensures the meeting results in:
- An agreed transformation scenario
- Named stakeholders (business + IT)
- Commitment to co-create a roadmap

---

### 6.2 Cross-CSA (STU) Actions – Post-Meeting

The AE pulls in CSA/STU to:
- Translate use cases into architectural thinking
- Validate platform readiness gaps
- Shape a multi-use-case roadmap (not a pilot)

---

### 6.3 ATS (Partner) Actions – Post-Meeting

Partners are engaged to:
- Extend capacity or velocity
- Contribute industry or delivery depth
- Support roadmap realization

Partners come in **after** the outcome conversation is earned.
